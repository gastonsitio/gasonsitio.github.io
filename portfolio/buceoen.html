Buceo

During 2020 pandemic along with Cristian Recoba we designed an identity system for Buceo, a neighbourhood from Montevideo, Uruguay.

We started our process by doing historical research and observing how the way to inhabit the area has changed from a more communal experience to an individualistic and fragmented way. The concepts we take to develop Buceo identity are totally based on this research. In order to summarize the process and not make the reading so long, the complete investigation and argumentation can be consulted in the following link.

“¡El Buceo!... ¿Quién no conoce, de la vieja y moderna generación, ese lugar histórico, cuyo pie bañan las aguas del Plata salado, y cuyos médanos fueron testigos silenciosos del desembarco de los ingleses, (...) como de las trifulcas de la Guerra Grande, cuando los cacherulos de Garibaldi lo visitaron? Sí, el tal Buceo es más conocido que la ruda, y mucho más...”

Isidoro de María, Montevideo Antiguo tomo II, 1887.

Conceptualization

With conceptualization we refer to the ideas we are interested to communicate through this brand, those values we try to recognize and replicate. They come from the same place but are distinguishable from each other to find new places that allow us to find those discursive relationships we would like to say to whoever is watching.


"Art to the rescue of the wreck"

To rescue the values found in the depths of Buceo. To recover, as if we were divers, the belongings that has been left at the bottom of the sea. To bring to the surface what we don't want to lose, before the ship sinks completely.

Urban fragmentation as that ship that wreck and sinks. Art and history as a reminder of what we were as a reflection of what we are, and as a seed of what we want to become.

Discuss society paradigms: Is the city being built today the one we want to live in the future? Public space as comfort zone. A safe place for people creativity. Reclaim our community sense.

"Appropriating public space as a creative tool for the community and memory"

‘Appropriating public space’ as an element of action and sovereignty of the neighbors. To feel part of a community.

‘Creative tool’ as a suggestive of cultural activities, exchanges, relationships, meetings, conversations, knowledge construction. To realize the creative potential of the community.

‘For the community and memory’ defines the purpose, the motivation, which is to build community ties in the current context without forgetting the memories. To build from our history.

Brand identity

The isotype is the central element of the visual identity system and constitutes the fundamental piece from which the entire visual system emerges.

The arch shape comes from a constant element used in 'Museo Oceanográfico' arquitecture, an emblematic building in Buceo. We take this element to use it as the main identity sign of our system.



Strategy

We designed a plan to start with brand presence in the area. This plan is divided into three phases looking for the permeability of the identity system in community and territory.

Problems:
• Disintegration of community in social life.
• Absence of neighbor/neighborhood/territory communication.
• Unawareness of cultural and historical value.

Objectives

General:
• Develop an open space for neighbors and communal organization. Where they could respond to communication, cultural and social needs of people.

Specific:
• Think about contemporary living, which ways of making, inhabiting and consuming the city.
• Strengthen dialogue between neighbors.
• Promote the participation and political decision of neighbors.
• Propose cultural and educational activities.
• Centralize communication.

Actions

PHASE 1: Teaser campaign

Make the brand known through a teaser campaign that shows a small part of the visual system with bait messages that invites people to visit the landing website. Here we want to generate curiosity, poeticize, problematize, question, interpellate.

• Paste posters on street and advertising on social media. Campaign starts.
• Website launch. Campaign landing page.
• Social media launch.
• Audiovisual content.

PHASE 2: Festival - "LA SALIDA" (the way out)

We want to celebrate and stimulate culture, recognize history, motivate public art and commemorate what is going to be a long process through a weekend festival.

Event program: Dances, DJs, rap battles, music, tributes, presentations, food, artisan fair and stencil activity with the prompt "what does Buceo means for you?".

• Festival brand, communication, posters, archigraphy, signage, merchandising.
• Schedule / programming / lineup / editorial.
• Animation / audiovisual.

PHASE 3: Permanent communication

It is the visual system attending daily aspects of the neighborhood, giving consolidation and final integration to the organization.

• Buceo Community website where participation and communication are encouraged.
• Social networks.
• "Mi barrio Buceo" magazine in homage to the old homonymous magazine.
• Meetings / meetings / workshops.
• Merchandising -> clothes (volunteers).
• Signage, map of housing complexes, neighborhood map with shops.
• "La casa del vecino" ("the neighbor's house", place where all of this will happen).
